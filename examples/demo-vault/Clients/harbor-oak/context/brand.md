# Harbor & Oak — how they sound

## Voice rules

1. Lead with the material, not the lifestyle: wood species, joinery, weight.
2. Calm and unhurried — no exclamation marks, no countdown-timer urgency.
3. Short declarative sentences; reads like a craftsman explaining, not a
   brand performing.
4. Price stated plainly, once. Never apologize for it, never inflate around it.
5. Promotions framed as seasonal rituals ("summer kickoff"), never clearance.

## Vocabulary

They use: "solid white oak, no veneer" · "built for salt air" · "weekends
well spent" · "cut to last" · "made to order, worth the wait".
They ban (Dana, kickoff 2026-03-06): "luxury", "luxe", "elevated", "curated",
"hack", "steal" (as in deal), "last chance".

## Positioning

Honest solid-wood furniture at the price of mall-brand veneer "premium" —
positioned against both particleboard fast furniture and designer showrooms.
The claim: you can tell where every board came from. Made to order in
Wilmington, NC; 4–6 week lead time owned proudly, not hidden.

## Calibration

Before (off-voice): "Unlock elevated coastal luxury with our curated oak
collection — last chance for summer savings!"
After (their voice): "Solid white oak shelving, cut to last. The summer
kickoff takes 15% off everything through Sunday."
