# Harbor & Oak — who they sell to

## Their customer

Homeowners 32–58, coastal and coastal-aspirational suburbs (Carolinas,
mid-Atlantic, New England), household income $120k+. Furnishing a renovated
room or a second place near the water; buys 1–2 statement pieces, then
returns for matching smaller pieces. ~38% of revenue is repeat (Shopify,
March pull).

## The problem in the customer's words

"Everything online is either particleboard junk or designer prices." ·
"I've been burned by 'solid wood' that turned out to be veneer." · "I don't
mind waiting if it's actually built when I order it."

## Buying triggers

Renovation finishing, a move near the coast, replacing a fast-furniture
piece that failed within two years, gift/registry moments (May–June bump).

## Objections heard in meetings

- "Is the wood really solid all the way through?" — top CS ticket theme,
  raised by Priya 2026-05-26; answered with construction close-ups in ads.
- 4–6 week lead time scares first-time buyers — Priya, 2026-04-17; mitigated
  in the welcome flow ("made when you order it") rather than hidden.
- Shipping cost on large pieces — freight fear; free-shipping threshold
  testing parked until after the summer sale (Dana, 2026-05-26).
