# ICP — who we serve

## Primary ICP

Founder or head of ecommerce at a DTC consumer brand, $2M–$20M revenue,
home/furniture/kitchen/apparel categories, team of 5–40. They own a real
product with real margins (55%+ contribution) and a paid program that used
to work.

Day-to-day reality: they are the bottleneck on creative approvals, they read
their dashboard daily but distrust it, and their last agency sent 40-slide
decks nobody opened.

The problem in their words: "We're spending more and getting less." ·
"I can't tell what the agency actually did last week." · "Our email list is
huge and does nothing."

Triggers that start the search: a CAC spike that survives two fixes, an
agency contract ending, the in-house marketer quitting, or a board/spouse
asking what the ad budget returns.

What they've tried first: boosting posts themselves, a big-name agency on
percent-of-spend, one freelancer per channel with nobody owning the blend.

## Real examples

- Harbor & Oak — coastal furniture brand, classic fit: founder-led, approval
  bottleneck, strong margins, list bigger than its email revenue.
- Alder Lane Bedding — Q1 2026 lost deal; right profile, but at $800k revenue
  they were a year early. Stays on the nurture list.

## Disqualifiers

- Under $1M revenue or under 50% contribution margin — efficiency work can't
  save the unit economics.
- Dropshippers and aggregator-owned brands — no voice, no patience for 90 days.
- Anyone who wants daily check-in calls — our model is weekly, written, on the
  record.
