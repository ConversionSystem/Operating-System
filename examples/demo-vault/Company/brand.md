# Brand — how we sound, where we stand

## Voice rules

1. Plain words over jargon. If a client's intern wouldn't get it, rewrite it.
2. Numbers over adjectives: "CAC down 9% in 3 weeks" beats "strong month".
3. Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
4. Confident, never salesy. Show the math instead of making promises.
5. Name the bad news first when there is bad news. Clients smell burying.
6. Signature phrase, used sparingly: "show the receipt".
7. Banned words: game-changer, unlock, supercharge, synergy, crushing it,
   best-in-class, holistic, leverage (as a verb).

## Positioning

We are the growth agency that reports like a CFO. Instead of a big-agency
retainer where the deck gets thicker as the numbers get worse, every claim we
make traces to a ledger line the client can grep. The enemy: the black-box
retainer — percent-of-spend pricing, vanity dashboards, and "brand lift" as
an excuse for rising CAC.

## Proof points

- Maya ran paid acquisition in-house at a $40M DTC home brand for 4 years;
  her team held blended CAC flat through iOS 14.5 while spend doubled.
- Brightline's pricing has never been percent-of-spend — incentives on
  efficiency since day one.
- Every client report since founding has shipped with ledger-sourced numbers;
  zero decks over 1 page.

## Calibration examples

Before: "We're thrilled to share another incredible month of wins across the
funnel — engagement is up and the brand is on fire!"
After: "ROAS held at 2.4 while CPMs rose 12%. CAC is up $6 in three weeks —
here's the cause and the two fixes we're running."

Before: "Our best-in-class creative engine will unlock untapped audiences."
After: "We ship 3–5 new ad variants a week. Last month 2 of 14 beat the
control; those two now carry 60% of spend."
