# Style — report

Identity: a CFO's memo, not a marketing deck.

## Rules

1. Evidence first: every claim traces to a ledger line, a date, or a file.
2. No adjectives without numbers — "strong week" is banned; "CPL down $11"
   is the whole point.
3. Bad news leads its section, with cause and the fix already in motion.
4. Tables for metrics, prose for meaning. Never a chart where a number does.
5. "No new data" is an honest cell; an estimate dressed as a reading is not.
6. Each section scannable in 10 seconds; the whole report in 2 minutes.
7. End with asks, each owned and dated.

## Example

CPL plainly: up $11 in two weeks (74 → 85). Cause: a competitor entered the
auction, not budget drift. Fix: two new angles entered testing Tuesday; first
read Friday. If neither beats control by next report, we shift 20% of budget
to the search campaigns that held CPL flat.
