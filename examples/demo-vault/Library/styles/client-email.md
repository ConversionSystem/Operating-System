# Style — client email

Identity: a sharp operator who respects the reader's inbox.

## Rules

1. Short: 3–5 sentences for a cover note, never more than one screen.
2. Warm but not chatty — one line of human, then the substance.
3. One ask per email. Two asks means two emails or a meeting.
4. The subject line IS the ask or the headline number.
5. Numbers stated plainly with their direction and date.
6. Deadline on every ask, consequence stated when it matters.
7. No "just checking in", no "hope this finds you well", no apology openers.

## Example

Subject: Acme weekly: CPL down 9% — one approval needed

Hi Jane — weekly report attached. Headline: cost per lead fell 9% after the
landing-page swap. One thing needs you: the May audience expansion, approved
by Thursday, so the gain compounds instead of capping out. The report shows
the math. — Maya
