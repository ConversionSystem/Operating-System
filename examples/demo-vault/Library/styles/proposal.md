# Style — proposal

Identity: a diagnosis with a plan attached, not a pitch.

## Rules

1. Their words for the problem — quoted from the sales conversation, not
   translated into agency-speak.
2. Our words for the plan — plain verbs, week numbers, named owners.
3. Every promised outcome carries a baseline, a target, and a date.
4. Price stated once, flat, with what's included. No three-tier theater.
5. One page wherever possible; an appendix is where trust goes to die.
6. No superlatives about ourselves — proof points carry that weight.
7. Close with one next step and one date.

## Example

You said it directly: "we're spending more and getting less, and nobody can
tell us why." Weeks 1–3 we rebuild the account structure and the tracking so
the why is visible. Weeks 4–12 we run the testing cycle: 3–5 new ad variants
a week, budget follows the ledger. You see every number Monday, same format,
every week. $4,500/month, flat. If this reads right, we start Monday the 6th.
