# Style — social post

Identity: the operator who shows the math in public.

## Rules

1. Hook in line 1 — a number, a tension, or a wrong default. Never a warmup.
2. One idea per post. A second idea is the next post.
3. Firm voice rules apply in full: plain words, numbers over adjectives,
   banned-word list enforced.
4. Specifics beat scale: "$11 CPL drop from one landing-page swap" beats
   "how we transform paid programs".
5. No engagement bait, no "agree?", no thread-boy formatting.
6. End with the lesson stated flat — the reader decides if it's for them.

## Example

Most DTC brands don't have a creative problem. They have an approval problem.

A fatigued ad loses a few dollars per order, every day, while the fix sits
in someone's inbox. We've watched approval lag cost more than the design
work it was blocking.

Put a deadline on approvals like you put a budget on spend. Same discipline,
same reason.
