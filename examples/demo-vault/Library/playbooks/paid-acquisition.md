# Playbook — paid acquisition

Delivery guide for the paid-acquisition offer (Meta + Google, $4,500/mo
flat). Draft: steps reflect how Maya runs it today; quality bars firm up as
engagements harvest into it.

## When to run this

New paid-acquisition retainer, or an existing account whose blended CAC has
drifted up two consecutive months. Assumes spend of $20k–$150k/month and
contribution margin ≥55% — below that, fix economics before efficiency.

## Inputs

- Ad account admin access (Meta Business Manager, Google Ads) + Shopify
  collaborator account.
- 90-day platform exports and the client's margin model.
- Creative inventory: every asset run in the last 6 months, with spend.
- goals.md populated: roas/cac baselines confirmed in the wild, not quoted
  from a dashboard screenshot.

## Steps

1. Tracking sanity pass — owner: account lead. Quality bar: platform-reported
   orders within 5% of Shopify over the same 14 days; discrepancies explained
   in writing before any restructure.
2. Account restructure (weeks 1–3) — owner: account lead. Quality bar: ≤2
   campaigns per objective, consolidated ad sets, naming convention applied;
   no legacy campaign left running unaccounted.
3. Creative cadence stand-up — owner: account lead, designer briefed weekly.
   Quality bar: 3–5 net-new variants entering test each week, each with a
   stated hypothesis ("material close-up beats lifestyle for cold").
4. Weekly budget reallocation — owner: account lead. Quality bar: budget
   moves follow the ledger, not last-48h noise; every move ≥10% gets a
   one-line note in the client's decisions log.
5. Monday ledger append + report — owner: delivery marketer. Quality bar:
   roas/cac lines dated and noted; report numbers match the ledger exactly.
6. Fatigue watch — owner: delivery marketer. Quality bar: frequency >4 or
   CTR −20% from peak triggers a refresh brief the same week, with an
   approval deadline attached.

## Outputs

Restructured account, a running creative test log, weekly roas/cac ledger
lines, and a Monday report the client can audit line by line.

## Common failure modes

- Too many campaigns: learning fragments and nothing reaches significance.
- Pausing fatigued ads instead of refreshing — resets delivery learning and
  usually costs more than the fatigue did.
- Letting client creative approval gate the testing calendar with no
  deadline or fallback — the account pays for their inbox.
- Reporting platform ROAS without the blended check against Shopify.
