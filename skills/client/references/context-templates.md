# Client context/ templates

Shape guides for the client's own context files — same discipline as Company/
templates: synthesized prose, real data only, omit empty sections. All carry
`type: context`, `reviewed:`, `tags: [client, {slug}]`.

## context/brand.md — how THEY sound
**Voice rules** (3–7 testable lines, extracted from their site/samples/guide) ·
**Vocabulary** (phrases they use, words they ban — exact quotes) · **Positioning**
(what they claim, against what alternative) · **Calibration** (one short
before/after pair if samples exist). This file is the voice authority for every
deliverable; if it's thin, say so when drafting and ask for one sample.

## context/icp.md — who THEY sell to
Their customer: role + situation · the problem in the customer's words · buying
triggers · objections we've heard in meetings (quoted, dated). Update from
meeting logs — this file compounds fastest.

## context/offers.md — what THEY sell
One `##` per product/service: what, for whom, price point if known, how it's
positioned. Note where OUR engagement plugs in ("we drive demand for line 2").

## context/people.md — their side of the table
One `##` per person: role, decision power, what they care about, communication
preferences, watch-outs. Append dated one-liners from meetings ("2026-06-09:
prefers Looms over docs"). Politics live here, nowhere else.

## context/stack.md — their tools we touch
Table: system · what we do in it · access notes (NEVER credentials — name where
credentials live, e.g. "in their 1Password, shared vault 'Agency'"). Offboarding
reads this file to revoke cleanly.
